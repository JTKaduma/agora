# [Design] Agora Help Center: UI/UX Design #539

## Summary
Users need a self-serve help center to understand how to use Agora — from buying tickets to creating events and managing their account. This design covers the full design of the Help Center experience, including the landing page, topic navigation, and popular articles.

### Key Requirements
- **Help Center Landing Page (/help)**:
    - **Hero Section**: Headline "How can we help you?", a large search bar for help articles, and subtext "Browse topics below or search for answers".
    - **Topic/Category Grid**: Structured navigation for:
        - Getting Started
        - Buying Tickets
        - Creating & Managing Events
        - Payments & Refunds
        - Account & Settings
        - Stellar & Web3
        - Troubleshooting
    - **Category Cards**: Each card includes an icon, title, and article count (e.g., "8 articles").
    - **Popular Articles**: A dedicated section for frequently accessed help content.
    - **Support CTA**: "Still need help?" section linking to a contact form.
- **Mobile Responsiveness**:
    - Category grid collapses to 2 columns on tablet and 1 column on mobile.
    - Table of contents converts to a collapsible accordion on mobile devices.

### Acceptance Criteria
- Designs cover all pages and user flows.
- Full responsive support for desktop and mobile breakpoints.
- All interactive states (hover, focus, active, empty, error) are designed.
- Consistent with Agora's visual system (typography, color variables, spacing).
- Figma frames are organized by page with component annotations and a handoff-ready layout.
